# Antuni Landing Page Content

## Column 1 - Album Description
 ::center:: 
MARGENROT
Antuni

"Antuni" is the third album by an Armenian musician from Siberia, Lusia Kazaryan (Margenrot). In her work, Lusia addresses the issue of national identity and reinterprets traditional Armenian chants and chorales, weaving them into experimental electronic music as well as incorporating the sounds of her surrounding environment. Antuni is a genre of gusan song (gusans were Armenian folk singers and rhapsodes) – a story told by a homeless, eternal wanderer, exiled and deprived of homeland. The album's title also refers to the melancholic ballad Antuni ("The Song of the Wanderer") by the Armenian composer, folklorist, and monk Komitas. This work was dedicated to the victims of the Armenian Genocide of 1915. Lusia's new album is a reflection of her own existential experience: a wandering of the lonely soul amid numerous contemporary wars. She asks an urgent, personal question: "What is my home, and where is it?"

## Column 2 - Rosa 15 Description

Rosa 15 is a small independent book and music publishing house with a curated selection, whose goal is to preserve unique, often rare and archival texts by Russian-language authors on poetry, contemporary art, anthropology, philosophy, and musical works, as well as to popularize the heritage of Soviet and post-Soviet culture beyond its geographical homeland.
